Switchboard

Product Design

Switchboard is a live chat app tightly integrated with our customer data that replaced a third-party service. While interviewing and shadowing our Customer Service team, I noticed a LOT of context switching while chatting. CSR's were referencing Word documents for responses they've customized, repeatedly clicking through different chats to be sure they've responded to everyone and creating a ton of browser tabs as they tried to find customer info.

As our dev team built the back-end, I designed and coded a new front-end chat layout that also displays customer data including previous chats, a complete website history, a live view of the customer’s cart, order statuses and more. In addition to the design and front-end development, I helped build and vet our feature roadmap.

An average chat length before Switchboard was 16.5 minutes and 12 messages. Conversations are now down to an average of 9.5 minutes and 8.5 messages. Some of the great features planned for the future include inline product previews, tracking conversions post chat, advanced order actions for CSR’s and some data visualization from our stats. FAST International

Visual Design & Front-End Development

This was a contract project with a local design studio. My process (from left to right below) ALWAYS starts with sketching. I try to get my bad ideas out on paper so that I can move faster in pixels or code. After I landed on some concepts that I liked (and that fit the client's preferences), I put together type, color and nav/header styles. I typically iterate through multiple ideas and fill out my sketched wireframes in Photoshop or Sketch. Once I have somethings close to a final direction, I'll start coding. Sometimes I'll work out a new idea right in my code editor, or other times I'll switch back and finalize it in pixels first. My final deliverables were client approved flats, responsive global site styles & markup and a handful of custom designed pages.
